Role-Level Onboarding with Peter

A short, role-specific guide to getting value from Peter (your AI COO) and the PF Operations Platform. Pick your role below. New team members can start here and learn at their own pace.

Executive (CEO / Partner)

For Brad and the partners — strategic oversight, fast answers, delegation.

What Peter does for you

  • Gives you a single, honest view of the business — pipeline, revenue, cash, projects
  • Researches and verifies anything before it reaches you, so you decide on facts
  • Takes legwork off your plate by delegating to specialist agents
  • Flags risks, deadlines, and opportunities you should know about

Your home base

  • CEO Dashboard — revenue vs goal, win rate, cash position, active projects
  • Pipeline — every bid and where it stands
  • Budget & Forecast and Invoicing & AR — the money view

Common things to ask Peter

Are we on pace to our $6M goal, and what's the gap?
Pull everything on this GC before my call — history, who decides, how they pay.
Research this opportunity and tell me if it's worth chasing — verified.
What's outstanding in AR and who's slow to pay?

How to get the most from him

  • Tell him the outcome you want and let him find the path
  • Correct him when he's off — he remembers and improves
  • Trust but verify — he shows his sources so you can

Estimating & Operations

For Jonathan and the bidding/ops side — feasibility, bids, submittals, delivery.

What Peter does for you

  • Reads geotech reports and gives an AP feasibility read (go / pass / needs-Garbin)
  • Pulls comparable past jobs so you bid from history, not guesses
  • Tracks bids through the pipeline and flags due dates
  • Helps chase submittals and shop drawings from GCs (recurring reminders)

Your home base

  • Feasibility / No-Go Tool and Bid Pipeline
  • Estimating and Material Costs
  • QA/QC — GUHMA, Modulus Testing, and Active Projects

Common things to ask Peter

Here's a new geotech report — is this an aggregate-pier job or a pass?
What did we charge and see on similar soils near this site?
Draft a feasibility response to the GC, flagging what Garbin needs to confirm.
Remind me to chase the shop drawings on this job every few days until they're in.

Where Peter stops

  • He gives the initial read; Dr. Garbin / GGG owns the engineering sign-off
  • He cites the report — verify anything that drives a real bid

Business Development

For Derek — finding work, knowing the players, opening doors.

What Peter does for you

  • Researches GCs, developers, and decision-makers — verified contacts
  • Maps data-center and commercial building opportunities in our regions
  • Profiles competitors so you know how to win the room
  • Drafts outreach emails and follow-ups in PF's voice

Your home base

  • BD / CRM contacts and Intel
  • Pipeline — what's in flight and what's coming

Common things to ask Peter

Who are the top 5 GCs building data centers in Indiana, and who runs procurement?
Build me a target list for this region with the right contact at each.
Draft a first outreach email to this GC — short, professional, in our voice.
What's the latest on the Google / Meta / AWS projects near us?

How to get the most from him

  • Tell him the type of work and region you want — he'll find the players
  • He verifies before naming a contact, so you don't chase ghosts

Admin / Office

For office and admin staff — data, documents, billing, and keeping things tidy.

What Peter does for you

  • Keeps project and bid data organized and findable
  • Surfaces invoicing, AR, and retainage so nothing slips
  • Files and organizes documents so any partner can find anything
  • Generates reports and summaries on request

Your home base

  • Invoicing & AR and Active Projects
  • Documents — User Manual, guides, and reports (all in-platform)
  • Change Orders, Permits & Inspections, Closeout

Common things to ask Peter

What invoices are outstanding and how old are they?
Pull together the closeout documents we still need for this project.
Summarize where each active project stands this week.
Where do I find the QuickBooks setup guide?

Good to know

  • Everything lives in the platform — you don't need to leave the page
  • Ask Peter in plain English; he'll point you to the right place or just do it
