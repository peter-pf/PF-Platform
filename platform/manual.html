Getting Started

The PF Operations Platform is your single home for everything Pier Foundations — bidding, estimating, projects, the field, and the books. Everything lives in one portal; there are no separate tools to log into.

This manual walks through every module: what it's for, who uses it most, what you can do, and a practical example to try.

Logging In

Everyone, first visit only

Open pf-platform.pages.dev/platform/. Your browser will pop up a small sign-in box. Enter the username and password your administrator gave you, then click sign in. Your browser remembers it, so you usually only do this once per device.

Try thisBookmark pf-platform.pages.dev/platform/ on your phone's home screen so it opens like an app.

Finding Your Way Around

Everyone

The layout is built to stay out of your way:

  • Dark sidebar on the left — your main menu, grouped into collapsible sections (Overview, Bidding, Financial, Field Operations, Documents). Click a heading to expand or collapse it.
  • Top bar — shows the platform name and a live sync status indicator on the right.
  • On your phone — the sidebar tucks away and a bottom tab bar gives you one-tap access to the modules you use most.

How the Data Works

Everyone — important to understand

The platform doesn't store its own copy of the numbers. It pulls live data from SharePoint — specifically the four master spreadsheets the team already maintains (bid log, projects, estimates, financials). When someone updates a spreadsheet, the platform reflects it on the next sync.

Look at the top right for "SharePoint Live | Synced: [date]". That timestamp tells you how fresh the data is. If a number looks off, check the sync time first — it may just need a refresh.

Try thisGlance at the sync timestamp every morning. If it's from a previous day, refresh the page to pull the latest from SharePoint.

The Quick-Action Button

Field crews & PMs especially

A round blue + button floats in the bottom-right corner of every screen. Tap it for fast access to the things you do most without hunting through the menu:

  • New Daily Log
  • Quick Bid Score
  • Safety Report
  • New Change Order
Try thisFrom a jobsite, tap the blue + → New Daily Log to file the day's report in under a minute.

Bidding

From Lead to Proposal

3 Feasibility Tool

Used most by: Jonathan & Derek — run on every new bid invite

What it's for: Score a bid before you spend estimating time on it. Enter the project details and the tool returns a verdict and a 0–100 score.

The verdict

BID pursue it   REVIEW worth a closer look   NO-BID walk away

Key things you can do
  • Get an instant 0–100 fit score from project inputs
  • See auto-flagged disqualifiers — peat soil, depth over 35 ft, and other deal-breakers raise a red flag automatically
  • Decide fast whether the opportunity deserves real effort
Try thisThe moment a bid invite lands, open Feasibility first. If it comes back NO-BID, you've just saved a day of estimating.

Putting It Together

A Day in the Life

The modules aren't islands — they hand work off to each other across the life of a project. Here's how they connect.

1
Morning

Open the Dashboard for the daily snapshot, then the CEO Dashboard for the KPIs and smart alerts. Confirm the SharePoint sync timestamp is current.

2
A new bid comes in

Run the Feasibility Tool first. If it says BID, add it to the Bid Pipeline, build the numbers in Estimating, price the stone in Material Costs, and send the proposal.

3
Project awarded

Set the job up in Active Projects, confirm Permits and locates, and schedule the rig in Equipment.

4
During install

Field files Daily Logs and Safety records, while QA/QC GUHMA and Modulus Testing capture proof that the columns meet design.

5
When scope changes

Open a Change Order, price it, and submit to the GC within 48 hours — no extra work until it's approved.

6
Project ends

Run Closeout (as-builts + GUHMA + modulus → GGG review), then send the final invoice and track it in Invoicing & AR.

7
Always, in the background

Data syncs from the SharePoint master spreadsheets automatically — so the Dashboard, financials, and project numbers stay current without anyone re-entering them.
