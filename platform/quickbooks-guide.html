Section 3

Job Costing Setup

3.1 Customer:Job Hierarchy

In construction QuickBooks, each project is a Job under a Customer. This is what makes every cost and dollar of revenue traceable to a specific project.

  • Customer = the general contractor or owner (e.g., "Weigand Construction").
  • Job = the specific project (e.g., "Weigand Construction:Project Zodiac — Fort Wayne").
  • Every invoice, bill, check, and timesheet must be tagged to the Customer:Job. No transaction goes un-jobbed.
  • Use a consistent naming convention: GC Name : Project Name — City. This keeps reports legible and matches the platform's pipeline naming.
Discipline Code costs to the job at the time of entry, not in a batch later. Batch-coding from memory is where job-cost accuracy goes to die.

3.2 Mapping Cost Codes to Estimate Line Items

The platform's master estimate produces line items by cost code. In QuickBooks, mirror this with Items (Service / Inventory-type items) that each point to the matching 5000-series account.

3.3 Labor Burden Setup

Flag PF's current 18% labor burden is very likely understated. For a specialty foundation contractor with high-risk workers' comp class codes, true burden is typically 35–45% once workers' comp is included. An 18% figure usually captures only payroll taxes (FICA + FUTA + SUTA) and omits workers' comp entirely — which means every bid is under-costing labor by 15–30 percentage points and quietly eroding net profit.

Components of a correct burden

ComponentTypical rate
FICA (Social Security + Medicare)7.65%
FUTA0.6%
SUTA (Indiana)2.5–5.4%
Workers' Comp (foundation work — high-risk class)8–15%
General Liability (labor portion)2–4%
PTO / Holiday3–5%
Health InsurancePer employee (est. $500–800/mo)
Total burden (with workers' comp)35–45%

How to set it up in QuickBooks

  • Enable payroll item tracking so employer taxes post automatically to the job via timesheets.
  • Add a burden cost item (or use QB's "billable time + costs" with a markup) to load workers' comp, GL, and PTO onto job labor.
  • Recalculate burden by class code semi-annually using actual workers' comp rates from Brad, and feed that figure back into the platform's estimate template.

3.4 Equipment Cost Allocation

PF owns its rigs (~$1.63M in fixed assets). To get true job profitability, charge an internal equipment rate to each project based on hours on-site.

# Internal hourly rate per major unit
Ownership cost/hr = (Depreciation + Insurance + Storage) / Annual hours available
Operating cost/hr = (Annual fuel + maintenance) / Annual hours used
Internal rate     = Ownership cost/hr + Operating cost/hr

# Example — Cat 336 (~$600K asset, 7-yr life)
Ownership ≈ $96,700/yr  ÷ 1,500 hrs = $64.47/hr
Operating ≈ $45–60/hr
Internal rate ≈ $110–125/hr
  • Log equipment hours per project (date, project, hours, operator).
  • Post the internal rate to the job (debit job equipment cost, credit an equipment-recovery account) so each project carries its real equipment cost.
  • Keep depreciation, insurance, and maintenance in the 6200–6500 overhead accounts; the internal rate is what allocates them to jobs.
Section 4

Progress Invoicing & WIP

4.1 Progress Invoicing Setup

  1. Create an Estimate for each project — the full contract value, broken into line items by Item/cost code.
  2. Turn on progress invoicing (Edit → Preferences → Jobs & Estimates → "Create invoices from a list of multiple estimates" / progress invoicing).
  3. To bill, create an Invoice from the Estimate and select a percentage or specific amount to bill this period.
  4. QuickBooks tracks original estimate, billed-to-date, and remaining automatically.

4.2 Retainage Tracking (5–10%)

Retainage is money earned but withheld until completion. It must be tracked separately, not buried in A/R.

  • On each progress invoice, add a negative "Retainage" line item (5–10%) that posts to 1210 Retainage Receivable.
  • This moves the withheld amount out of regular A/R so aging reports stay accurate.
  • When retainage is released, reclassify it from 1210 back to 1200 Accounts Receivable, then collect.
# At billing (10% retainage on $166,000)
DR 1200 Accounts Receivable     $149,400
DR 1210 Retainage Receivable     $16,600
   CR 4000 Contract Revenue              $166,000

# At retainage release
DR 1200 Accounts Receivable      $16,600
   CR 1210 Retainage Receivable          $16,600
Cash impact At $6M revenue and 10% retainage, up to $600K can be tied up at once. Track release dates per contract and submit closeout docs immediately at substantial completion — retainage is earned money, not a bonus.

4.3 Producing a WIP Schedule

QuickBooks does not produce a Work-in-Progress schedule natively. Build it by exporting QB job data and adding cost-to-complete:

  1. Run Job Profitability Detail (revenue + actual cost per project) and export to Excel monthly.
  2. Run Estimates vs. Actuals for current cost estimates.
  3. Add columns: Estimated Total Cost, Cost-to-Complete, % Complete, Earned Revenue, Over/(Under) Billed.
  4. Update Cost-to-Complete from the project manager (the field, not accounting) each month.

Section 7

Tax-Smart Account Setup

7.1 Set These Up Now to Support the Tax Strategy

A few tracking decisions made at setup save thousands at tax time. Configure these from day one rather than reconstructing later.

Set upWhy it matters
Section 179 / bonus depreciation equipment tracking
Fixed-asset list with purchase date, cost, in-service date per unit (1500/1510/1520)		 OBBBA permanently restored 100% bonus depreciation and raised Section 179 to ~$2.5M. PF's $1.63M equipment base can be fully expensed — but only with clean per-asset records and in-service dates.
Off-road diesel fuel tracking
Sub-account or class to separate clear (undyed) off-road diesel in 5210 / 6500		 Federal fuel tax credit of $0.243/gallon for clear diesel burned in off-road equipment (Form 4136). At 20K–40K gal/yr off-road, that's $4,860–9,720/yr — a dollar-for-dollar credit. Requires usage logs separating off-road from on-road.
R&D expense tracking
Class or tag for qualifying activities (installation-method development, GUHMA software, test columns)		 VSC technique development, the GUHMA QA/QC software, and test-column work likely qualify for the Section 41 R&D credit (~7–10% of qualifying spend). Post-2025, domestic R&E is also immediately deductible — a double benefit. Needs contemporaneous records.
Multi-state tracking
Class/location per state: IN, OH, MI, IL, KY, WI		 Sending crews and equipment into a state creates nexus and filing obligations. Illinois has no reciprocity with Indiana; Ohio has the CAT gross-receipts tax. Tag revenue and payroll by state at entry so multi-state returns are mechanical, not forensic.
De minimis safe harbor & capitalization policy
Written policy expensing items under $2,500/invoice		 A written capitalization policy in place at the start of the tax year lets PF immediately expense small tools and parts under $2,500 — simpler books, faster deductions.
How to track in QuickBooks Use Classes (QB Desktop/Online) for state and for R&D so a single transaction can be both job-costed and tax-tagged. Keep the Fixed Asset Item List meticulous for depreciation. Have a construction-specialized CPA confirm the R&D credit and multi-state filings — these are high-value, documentation-dependent positions.
