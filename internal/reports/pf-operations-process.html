Recommended Additions

01 Bid/No-Bid Decision Criteria High
Currently there is no formal process for deciding whether to invest time in a bid. Estimating effort takes 20+ hours per bid. Without clear go/no-go criteria, PF could waste resources on bids they should not be chasing.
What to add: A checklist before committing to bid -- evaluate project distance from yard, GC relationship history, project size relative to PF capacity, schedule conflicts with active jobs, bonding capacity, competition level.
02 Geotech Report Disqualification Criteria High
The SOP says "confirm the project requires AP" but does not define what makes a project NOT suitable. A new estimator would not know when to pass.
What to add: Specific soil conditions that disqualify AP (peat deposits, very soft clay cu < 15 kPa, treatment depths > 30 ft, clean dense sands that do not need improvement). Include SPT N-value and cu ranges that indicate AP is viable.
03 RFI Process During Bidding Medium
Geotech reports are often incomplete, drawings have conflicts, or specs are ambiguous. No step exists for asking questions during the bid period.
What to add: Step for identifying questions during geotech/drawing review, submitting RFIs to GC before bid date, documenting responses, and adjusting estimate accordingly.
04 Competitive Pricing Sanity Check Medium
No step for verifying the estimate is in a competitive range. PF could be significantly over or under market without knowing.
What to add: Quick check against industry benchmarks ($15-20/LF for stone columns, $1,200-$3,000 per pier). Compare $/LF and $/column to recent PF bids on similar projects. Flag estimates that are more than 15% above or below typical range for review.
05 Margin and Markup Guidance High
The SOP covers cost building but never mentions target margins, how to adjust for risk, or pricing strategy.
What to add: Target margin range for standard projects. Adjustments for risk factors: new GC (+X%), difficult site access (+X%), tight schedule (+X%), prevailing wage projects (+X%), high-profile projects (strategic pricing guidance).
06 Insurance and Bonding Verification Medium
GCs often require specific insurance limits and bonding. If PF does not have adequate coverage for a project, they cannot bid or win.
What to add: Step to check GC's insurance requirements (per spec/bid docs) against PF's current coverage. Verify bonding capacity for the bid amount. Contact bonding company if project exceeds current capacity.
07 Prevailing Wage / Certified Payroll Check High
Public projects (DOT, municipal, federally funded) require prevailing wages which significantly increase labor costs. Missing this wrecks the estimate.
What to add: Step to check if the project is publicly funded (DOT, municipality, IIJA-funded). If yes, look up prevailing wage rates for the project county and adjust labor costs in the estimate.
08 Addenda Tracking Log Low
Multiple addenda can come in during the bid period. Without a formal tracking system, changes get missed and the estimate is wrong.
What to add: Simple addenda log -- addenda number, date received, what changed, was Dr. Ed notified (Y/N), was estimate updated (Y/N).
09 Bid Day Submission Checklist Medium
No step covers the actual submission process -- who gives final approval, when to submit, how to submit, confirmation of receipt.
What to add: Bid day checklist -- final number review (who signs off), submission method (email, portal, BuildingConnected), deadline with timezone, confirmation of receipt from GC, follow-up call within 24 hours.
10 Price Escalation Language in Proposal High
With 50% steel tariffs and 4-6% aggregate cost increases in 2026, material costs can change between bid and mobilization. Without escalation protection, PF absorbs the increase.
What to add: Standard price escalation clause in the proposal template. Recommend ConsensusDocs 200.1 language. Include expiration date on pricing (30-60 days typical).

Recommended Additions

01 Subcontract Review Process High
The SOP mentions "subcontract is fully executed" but does not cover reviewing the GC's subcontract terms. Unfavorable clauses around retainage, pay-when-paid, indemnification, or warranty can cost PF significantly.
What to add: Step to review subcontract before signing -- check payment terms, retainage percentage, insurance requirements, indemnification clauses, warranty period, liquidated damages, dispute resolution. Flag unfavorable terms for Brad's review. Consider having Dr. Ed or counsel review contracts over a certain dollar threshold.
02 Pre-Mobilization Site Visit High
Showing up to a site blind creates problems -- access too tight for equipment, overhead power lines, soft ground that will not support the excavator, no staging area for stone delivery.
What to add: Required site visit before mobilization for projects over a certain threshold (or all projects). Checklist: equipment access path and clearances, staging area for stone storage, overhead obstructions, underground utilities (call 811), water source (if wet method), nearest stone supplier delivery route.
03 Mobilization Equipment and Material Checklist Medium
Forgetting a piece of equipment or tooling means a crew sitting idle on site while someone drives back to the yard.
What to add: Standard mobilization checklist -- vibroflot and attachments, excavator, loader/skid steer, F450/F550 with trailer, job trailer, testing jack and gauges, dial indicators, reference beam, safety equipment, hand tools, daily log sheets, modulus test sheets, survey/layout stakes, water pump (if needed).
04 Crew Assignment and Certification Verification Medium
No process for confirming crew members are assigned and their certifications are current before sending them to a job site.
What to add: Step to assign crew (operator, laborer, spotter), verify OSHA 10/30 certifications are current, confirm equipment operator certifications, ensure any site-specific training (MSHA, confined space, etc.) is completed.
05 Pre-Construction Meeting with GC Medium
Alignment with the GC before mobilization prevents misunderstandings about schedule, access, coordination with other trades, and site rules.
What to add: Schedule pre-con meeting with GC PM to cover: schedule and sequencing, site access and staging, daily coordination protocol, safety plan review, weather day procedures, quality requirements, inspection/testing schedule, payment application process.
06 Insurance Certificate Delivery High
GCs require a Certificate of Insurance (COI) naming them as additional insured before allowing work to begin. If PF forgets this step, mobilization gets delayed.
What to add: Step to request COI from insurance agent with GC named as additional insured. Send to GC at least 1 week before mobilization. Verify limits match subcontract requirements.
07 Stone Delivery Coordination High
Stone needs to be on site when the crew arrives and needs continuous delivery during installation. No step covers this coordination.
What to add: Step to contact stone supplier 2 weeks before mobilization -- confirm pricing, schedule delivery start date, agree on daily delivery quantity (tons/day based on production rate), confirm delivery access and staging area, establish backup supplier contact.
08 Change Order Process High
Field changes happen on every project -- additional columns, depth changes, scope additions by GC. Without a formal CO process, PF does extra work without getting paid.
What to add: Step-by-step change order process -- field team identifies change, notifies PM immediately, PM documents the change with photos/logs, PM prices the change using estimate rates, PM submits CO to GC in writing within 48 hours, work does not proceed on changed scope until CO is approved (except emergency).

Recommended Additions

01 Acceptance Criteria for Short Columns High
The SOP flags short columns in red but does not define how short is acceptable. Is 3 inches short OK? 1 foot? Without clear thresholds, every short column becomes an escalation.
What to add: Define depth tolerance (e.g., columns within 6 inches of design depth are acceptable without review; columns more than 6 inches short require Dr. Ed's engineering review; columns more than 2 feet short require potential reinstallation). Get Dr. Ed to define these thresholds.
02 Pressure Data Interpretation Guide Medium
The GUHMA records flot pressure, winch down pressure, and winch up pressure continuously, but the SOP does not explain what these readings mean or what anomalies to look for.
What to add: Reference guide -- what does normal pressure look like during a good installation? What does a pressure spike indicate (hitting a hard layer, obstruction)? What does a pressure drop indicate (void, very soft zone)? What patterns suggest inadequate compaction? This turns the GUHMA from a recording tool into a diagnostic tool.
03 Stone Consumption Rate Verification Medium
Total bucket count only tells part of the story. Stone consumption per linear foot is a better indicator of column quality. Abnormally high consumption could indicate a void; low consumption could indicate inadequate compaction.
What to add: Calculate stone consumption rate (buckets per LF or tons per LF) for each column. Compare to expected rate from Dr. Ed's design. Flag columns with consumption more than 20% above or below expected.
04 QC Completion Timing Requirement High
If GUHMA review happens after the crew has demobilized, short columns cannot be fixed without remobilizing -- which is expensive.
What to add: Requirement that GUHMA column checking must be completed within 24 hours of final column installation, BEFORE the crew demobilizes. Short columns identified during review must be reinstalled before crew leaves site.
05 Failed Column Response Procedure High
When a column fails QC (short depth, missing buckets, anomalous data), there is no documented procedure for resolution.
What to add: Step-by-step: notify Jonathan immediately, send column data to Dr. Ed with specific question (accept as-built or reinstall?), document Dr. Ed's response, if reinstall needed -- coordinate with site crew before demobilization. If crew already left, document cost of remobilization.
06 Complete Closeout Document Package Medium
The SOP mentions sending the GUHMA PDF to GC and engineer, but does not list the full closeout package.
What to add: Closeout package checklist -- GUHMA column installation report (PDF), modulus test results, as-built column layout drawing (with actual depths noted), stone delivery tickets, daily installation logs, any change order documentation, warranty letter (if applicable).

Recommended Additions

01 Actual Test Procedure High
The current SOP covers setting up the test SHEET but not how to actually perform the physical test. An operator needs to know the loading sequence, hold times, and reading procedures.
What to add: Step-by-step test execution -- set up reaction frame and loading jack, install settlement measuring devices (dial gauges on independent reference beam), load in increments (25%, 50%, 75%, 100%, 150%, 200% of design load), hold each increment for specified time (typically 5 minutes or until settlement stabilizes), record settlement readings, unload in decrements, record rebound. Reference ASTM D1143 Loading Procedure B.
02 Pass/Fail Acceptance Criteria High
The SOP does not define what constitutes a passing or failing test. The operator and PM need to know immediately if a test passes.
What to add: Define acceptance criteria -- maximum settlement at design load (100%), maximum settlement at 200% design load, minimum stiffness modulus required (from Dr. Ed's design). Example: if settlement at design load exceeds X inches, the test fails. Get Dr. Ed to define project-specific thresholds based on his GI-100 design criteria.
03 Test Column Selection Criteria Medium
No guidance on which columns to test or how many.
What to add: Selection criteria -- test frequency (1 per 500 piers minimum, or as specified in the contract/submittal), select test columns in the weakest soil zone identified in the geotech report, avoid columns at the edge of the treatment area, select columns representative of different pier lengths if the design has varying depths. Coordinate test column selection with Dr. Ed.
04 Equipment Setup Procedure Medium
Accurate test results depend on proper setup. No guidance on reaction frame, jack positioning, or gauge placement.
What to add: Setup checklist -- clear the column head and level with grout/sand cap, center the loading plate on the column, set up the reaction system (weighted platform, anchor piles, or dead weight), install reference beam supported independently from the test column (minimum 8 feet from test column), mount dial gauges on the reference beam, verify jack is centered and plumb, take initial readings before applying any load.
05 Results Review and Reporting Medium
No step for who reviews test results or how they are documented beyond the test sheet.
What to add: After test completion -- calculate stiffness modulus from load-settlement data, compare to design modulus requirement, send results to Dr. Ed for review and approval, include approved results in the closeout document package, report results to GC within 48 hours of test.
06 Failed Test Response Procedure High
What happens if a column fails the modulus test is not documented.
What to add: If test fails -- immediately notify Jonathan and Dr. Ed, do NOT proceed with additional testing until failure is discussed, evaluate cause (installation issue, soil conditions, equipment malfunction), options include: retest on a different column, reinstall the failed column and retest, modify the design (additional columns, closer spacing), Dr. Ed makes the engineering call.
