Vibratory Stone Columns (VSC)

Vibratory stone columns are cylindrical columns of compacted aggregate installed into weak or compressible soils using a vibratory probe (vibroflot). As the probe penetrates the soil and aggregate is fed and compacted in lifts, the result is a dense column of stone that reinforces the ground and transfers structural loads to competent bearing strata.

The surrounding soil is simultaneously densified during installation, creating a composite ground mass with dramatically improved bearing capacity, reduced settlement, and enhanced drainage characteristics.

When to Use VSC

  • Soft or loose soils with low bearing capacity
  • Sites with high settlement potential
  • Projects where deep foundations are cost-prohibitive
  • Fast-track schedules requiring rapid ground improvement
  • Sites sensitive to noise and vibration
  • Projects exposed to steel tariff risk

Our Approach

How We Work

From site assessment to project closeout, every step is engineered for quality and efficiency.

01

Site Assessment

We review your geotechnical report, confirm aggregate pier requirements, and evaluate site conditions to determine the optimal ground improvement approach.

02

Engineering Design

Dr. Garbin designs the column layout, spacing, depths, and aggregate specifications tailored to your project's structural loads and soil conditions.

03

Mobilization

Equipment and crew deploy from our Fort Wayne yard. We maintain our own fleet for rapid mobilization -- typically on-site within days of contract execution.

04

Installation

The vibroflot penetrates to design depth and stone aggregate is compacted in lifts, creating dense columns that reinforce the soil. Production rates of 40-60 columns per day are typical.

05

Quality Verification

GUHMA data logging records installation parameters in real time. Modulus load testing confirms design bearing capacity is achieved on every project.

06

Closeout

Complete as-built documentation and QC reports are delivered. Our engineer remains available for any post-installation support or questions from your structural team.

Why Ground Improvement?

Side-by-side comparison of vibratory stone columns versus traditional deep foundations.

Feature VSC / Aggregate Piers Traditional Deep Foundations
Cost 30-50% less Baseline
Installation Speed 40-60 piers/day 5-10 piles/day
Noise & Vibration Minimal Significant
